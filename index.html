Required Skillset for Nursing

There are various skills that nurses require. The nursing field is greatly dependent on skills. They usually develop most of these skills during training. Those interested in taking up the course should analyse their expertise with the respective skillsets. Nurses require certain skills to work in this field. Here are a few:
Dexterity- Nurses need to have skillful hands. Since nurses have to perform various procedures that require them to be able to work well with their hands, dexterity is important. They should have stable hands and should be able to perform procedures and tests meticulously.
Stamina- Nurses work a lot more than doctors. They spend long hours at work. Most of the time, they have to stand through time-consuming operations. They are required to attend to patients regularly. For all this, they need physical strength to stand through physically exerting work.
Mental Frame- Nurses work in an environment that is constantly faced with deaths and other such negative and disappointing happenings. Nurses should be able to work in painful environments. Being the lowest in the chain, they usually hear the brunt of doctors and patients. To survive through all this, they should train themselves to have a strong mental frame.
Presence of Mind- Most of the work in the medical field are learnt and dealt with spontaneously. Training is usually insufficient to deal with matters that arise in the fields. Nurses should have the ability to react quickly to situations. For this presence of mind is important, they must know how to deal with problematic situations. They are sometimes required to work with patients when the doctor is unavailable also. Hence, presence of mind is important.
Knowledge- Besides the training that the nurses are provided with, they should have basic medical knowledge. They should be aware of various procedures and methods in the field. This is not for them to conduct operations, but so that they are prepared to deal with unexpected circumstances.
Observational skills- The training during nursing courses is learnt mostly through observing. The nursing students are required to observe the procedures conducted and learn from them. Their entire training is based majorly on observation.
Organisational skills- Nurses deal with a lot of responsibilities. For them to complete the work assigned to them on time, they need to be organised. They must have the ability to perform their work without making mistakes.

Course Curriculum for Nursing

The undergraduate course in nursing is usually for four years. During these years, the students are provided with adequate knowledge of various medical aspects required in the field. They are trained in various practical procedures and methods. They are given all-round training throughout the years. Colleges also provide students with internships. During the final years, the students are usually made to work in various medical colleges and government hospitals as part of their training. The postgraduate training is usually for two years. Postgraduate training gives more exposure to students. They gain more knowledge related to the field.
